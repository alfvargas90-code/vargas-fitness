# Hybrid·01 — Arc-Crowned Pillars  (Pillars + a temporal crown)

**One-line POV:** The Pillars grid, but with the Day Arc as its crown — three at-a-glance domain columns that now know *where in the day they sit*.

## The fusion
This is the gentlest, lowest-risk merge of 2 + 3. It keeps Pillars' core win — Recovery · Activity · Body all visible at once, no tabs — and swaps the moon-only crown for the **Day Arc**: a sun at dawn, the real moon at night, and a glowing "NOW · 12:30" marker on the arc. Then each column header carries an "as of" time tick (Recovery 06:40 · Activity now · Body 07:05), so the temporal layer is felt *inside* the grid too.

Spatial structure dominates; time is the framing context.

## Best for
The daily-glance user who wants the whole system in one screen **and** a quick sense of the day's shape — without giving up the column legibility. It's the most production-ready of the three: closest to today's app, least conceptually demanding.

## Trade-off
The arc is mostly orientation, not data — it tells you it's midday more than it plots anything. If you want the timeline to *carry* information, see 02 (matrix) or 03 (tracks).
