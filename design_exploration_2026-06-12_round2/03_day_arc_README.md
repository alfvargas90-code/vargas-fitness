# R2·03 — Day Arc / Timeline

**One-line POV:** Today as a journey from dawn to night — a sun-to-moon arc up top, then a vertical timeline spine where each metric is anchored to the hour it belongs to.

## The point of view
Reframes the dashboard from *categories* to *chronology*. A graceful SVG **day arc** spans the top card: a sun rising at the left (06:40 · dawn), the real moon seated at the night end, and a glowing **"NOW · 12:30"** marker on the arc just past midday. The arc uses the cyan→purple→coral gradient feel of the brand.

Below it, a **vertical timeline spine** runs down the page with time-stamped, phase-tinted glass nodes:
- **Morning · Recovery** (06:40) — ANS −4.2, sleep, HRV, RHR + a pink-italic read of how he woke.
- **Midday · Strain** (NOW) — steps, active burn, exertion; the current node, highlighted.
- **Evening · Fuel** (~18:00) — calories + macros, what's left to eat.
- **Night · Rest** — tonight's sleep target, the recovery environment, the moon.

It tells the body's data as the *shape of a day* rather than a set of drawers.

## What user it serves best
The user who thinks in **rhythm and timing** — when did I wake compromised, where am I now, what's the move tonight. It naturally answers "what's next" instead of just "what is," and the chronological framing makes a single day feel coherent and actionable.

## Trade-offs
- The arc up top is largely narrative/decorative — it spends real estate on metaphor.
- Longer vertical scroll than Hero-First or Pillars; less of a one-glance tool, more of a read-through.
