# R2·04 — Story / Cinematic Scroll

**One-line POV:** The day as a told story — a full-bleed moon curtain-raiser, then chapters that fade in on scroll, the pink-italic voice carrying you from one number to the next.

## The point of view
The most emotional take that still lives fully inside his aesthetic. It opens on a **full-size moon+rings hero** with a single line — *"A quiet day to recover"* — and a pink-italic opening that sets the tone for a compromised-recovery day. Then the page becomes **scroll-revealed chapters**, each a glass section with a colored eyebrow, one hero number, and connective italic prose that hands off to the next:

*Last night* (sleep) → *This morning* (the ANS/HRV emotional core, reframed gently) → *So far today* (strain) → *Fuel* (macros) → *The body* (weight/comp) → a closing read that ties it together.

Real motion: IntersectionObserver fades + lifts each chapter in, with a gentle parallax on the moon — and a `prefers-reduced-motion` path that disables all of it. The narrative voice is the through-line, not a grid.

## What user it serves best
The user who wants his data to **mean something**, not just display — a once-a-morning, sit-with-it ritual rather than a quick glance. It's the best home for the Currents prose engine he already has, and the gentlest way to deliver bad-recovery news without it reading as failure.

## Trade-offs
- Highest-effort direction: the copy has to be genuinely good every day or the cinema feels hollow — it leans hardest on the prose-generation layer.
- A long scroll with one idea per screen is the *opposite* of glanceability — wrong choice for a 5-second check, right choice for a morning read.
