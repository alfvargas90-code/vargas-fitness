# R2·02 — Pillar Columns

**One-line POV:** Kill the tabs. Recovery · Activity · Body stand as three vertical columns under one moon crown — the whole system legible at a glance, no switching.

## The point of view
The live app splits the body across three horizontal tabs (Active / Recovery / Body). This direction makes that mental model **spatial instead of temporal**: the real moon+rings spans the top as a shared crown (keeping its three floating metric corners), and beneath it sit **three narrow glass columns side-by-side**, each owning its accent — Recovery (pink/cyan), Activity (orange), Body (violet).

Each column is a tight vertical stack of metric→value rows with tabular numerals, a progress bar (steps), a sparkline (active cal). A full-width pink-italic "Today's Read" card anchors the bottom, with the sync stamps as pills. It's the iPad multi-column dashboard mental model, made to work at 375px.

## What user it serves best
**The system architect who wants the whole machine on one screen.** No tab-switching, no hidden state — recovery, output, and body composition are all in peripheral vision at once, comparable side-by-side. This is the most "see everything, trust nothing hidden" of the four, and the most structurally distinct from today's layout while staying 100% on-brand.

## Trade-offs
- Three columns at 375px means ~113px each — type must stay tight; it's elegant here but it's the layout most at risk of feeling cramped if metrics are added.
- Less emotional focus than Hero-First or Story — it's a control panel, not a narrative.
