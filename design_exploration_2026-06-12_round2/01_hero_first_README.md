# R2·01 — Hero-First

**One-line POV:** One enormous "metric of the day," dynamically chosen, answers *what should I do right now* — everything else collapses into a quiet ledger beneath it.

## The point of view
Same visual world as the live app — dark gradient, glass cards, the real moon+rings, pink-italic prose — but the hierarchy is ruthlessly singular. Instead of three coequal tab worlds, the screen leads with the **single most important number today**. Today that's the **ANS Charge −4.2 / Recovery Compromised**, rendered huge with its cyan glow, a one-word verdict, and a pink-italic line that tells him how to live the day. The moon shrinks to a corner signature inside the hero card.

Below the hero, the rest of the day becomes a **calm, scannable ledger** — Sleep / HRV / RHR / Weight / Activity / Nutrition as compact glass rows with pill deltas. Loud about the one thing that matters, quiet about the rest.

The "metric of the day" is meant to be **dynamic**: on a great-recovery day it'd headline strain or a workout; on a weigh-in day, body comp. The layout always promotes whatever is most decision-relevant now.

## What user it serves best
The user who opens the app for **a decision, not a browse** — "given everything, what's the move today?" It's the strongest answer to Alfredo's overanalysis tendency: it pre-decides the focus and demotes the noise. Emotionally smart too — it reframes a bad recovery number as guidance, not alarm.

## Trade-offs
- Needs a real "what matters most" ranking rule in code to pick the hero metric — a small engine, not just CSS.
- A pure system-thinker may dislike that the rest is demoted; pair it with Pillars' everything-visible lower section if he wants both.
