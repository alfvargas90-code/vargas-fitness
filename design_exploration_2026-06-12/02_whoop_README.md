# 02 · Whoop-Clone Refinement — "Recovery"

**One-line POV:** Three rings, one verdict. A near-black recovery screen where a single colour — green/amber/red — tells you everything before you read a number.

## The point of view
This direction commits hard to the Whoop visual grammar because it *works*: a glanceable, colour-coded recovery score that compresses a noisy autonomic picture into one decision. Today the hero **Recovery ring reads 41% in red** (driven by the −4.2 ANS Charge), with the colour legend (RECOVERED / ADEQUATE / **LOW**) making the semantics unmissable.

Design language:
- **Hero recovery ring** with a soft red glow, big number centred, "ANS Charge −4.2 · below baseline" as the subline.
- **Two secondary rings** — Sleep (85% of 8h target, blue) and Exertion/Strain (orange) — sized smaller, clearly subordinate.
- **Ruthless minimalism**: thin type, deep negative space, no decorative gradients, no cartoon cards.
- **Dense sparkline vitals** below — HRV, resting HR, ANS Charge trends in tight inline SVG, the "scroll for more" layer.

## What user it serves best
The **athlete in a training loop** who already knows what HRV and ANS mean and wants a daily go/no-go signal in under two seconds. The colour does the cognitive work; the sparklines reward a second look. This is the most *familiar* of the four — it reads as a premium fitness wearable app, which lowers the learning curve to zero.

## Trade-offs
- It is, openly, a clone of an existing visual identity — distinctive to the category but not to Alfredo.
- Compressing ANS Charge into a single 0–100% "recovery" number throws away nuance the raw value carries; risks over-simplifying a metric he clearly cares about precisely.
